<h1 id="about-the-author">About the Author</h1>

<p>David Silva is a Senior Software Engineer at Tembo Money, where he leads architectural modernisation of a mortgage recommendation platform — decomposing a large Rails monolith into modular engines and applying the exact patterns discussed in this book. He has over 15 years of experience building Ruby applications across fintech (Tembo Money, Creditspring), government services (GOV.UK, Ministry of Justice), and platforms serving over a million users (Indeed).</p>

<p>He is the founder of <a href="https://carernotes.uk">CarerNotes</a>, an AI-powered documentation platform that helps UK domiciliary care providers turn voice recordings into professional, CQC-compliant care notes. He built it as a Rails application with an offline-first Progressive Web App — applying many of the architectural principles discussed in this book.</p>

<p>His first book, <a href="https://www.amazon.co.uk/dp/B0G1RBWF6V"><em>Building Your Own Roguelike: A Practical Guide</em></a>, explores game development in pure Ruby using Entity-Component-System architecture and event-driven design — patterns that translate directly to the engine integration patterns in Chapter 8.</p>

<p>David writes about Ruby, software architecture, and the trade-offs that come with both at <a href="https://davidslv.uk">davidslv.uk</a>.</p>
