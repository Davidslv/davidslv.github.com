06 · Howfour simple rules
what makes it careful, not just fast

Four simple rules.

Everything you just saw was one of these four. Here they are.

firm-os07
Rule 01bigger risk, more checks
01

The bigger the risk,
the more checks.

Every check on a risky change. Nothing at all on a typo. Checks that slow you down every time just get switched off — so firm stays quiet on the small stuff and strict where it counts.

you saw it: the reviewers that stayed quiet, because the change was small.
answers: “won't this slow me down?”08
Rule 02one job, one owner
02

One job.
One owner.

Each job belongs to one helper — not a committee. It can work on its own because the rules are clear, and there's always one place the rule lives.

you saw it: the questions it forced up front, each reviewer speaking only for its own job.
firm-os09
Rule 03a stop beats a reminder
03

A hard stop beats a reminder.

Under pressure, “remember to check X” fails. Something that simply won't let you past does not. Automatic checks catch the obvious; a second reviewer catches the rest.

you saw it: firm reviewing its own code — and flagging a gap in its own rules.
firm-os10
Rule 04prove it helps
04

Prove it helps,
or drop it.

A rule you can't show helps is just an opinion. So every rule in firm has to earn its place.

next slide: how each rule earns its spot.

08 · The payoffit gets better over time

Every project teaches the next one.

A handy trick, a new rule, a word that's specific to our team — each one gets written down where the next session will read it. So the same mistake doesn't happen twice — and neither do you.
