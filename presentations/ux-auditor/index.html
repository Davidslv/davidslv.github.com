What that just did · in plain terms

A 🟡 isn't a tool dump.
It's a decision.

What happened
Five reviewers, at once

Speed, access, the real flow, the glance and the words — all reviewed in parallel, not one tool after another. If one stalls, the rest still report.

Then merged
Deduped & scored once

Overlapping findings collapse, then every signal is graded against one shared rules table — so the same page gets the same verdict every run.

What 🟡 means to ship
Safe to ship — fix 3 first

🟢 = ship as-is. 🟡 = nothing blocks the release, but three things are worth fixing. 🔴 = there's a must-fix; don't ship yet.

Why one verdict
A call, not five tabs

Five separate reports leave you to cross-reference and prioritise. One synthesised verdict has already done that — and tells you whether to ship.

The payoff

You point it at a URL and get back a ship / don't-ship decision with the evidence behind it — instead of five dashboards and a judgement call you make yourself, under deadline.

What each specialist actually catches
Five surfaces.
Five real findings.

Not capabilities — examples. One concrete finding per agent, in the exact shape it reports, with its plain meaning and the fix.

1 / 5 · perf-console — speed & Core Web Vitals
[ performance · worth-fixing ]
LCP 3,420 ms — above the 2.5 s "good" line
What it means: LCP (Largest Contentful Paint) is how long until the biggest thing on screen — usually the hero image — actually appears. Over 2.5 s the page feels slow, and first-time visitors start to bounce.
1Evidence · assets/lighthouse.json→Fix · preload the hero image, inline the LCP font-face

The rule: 2500–3999 ms is worth-fixing; ≥ 4000 ms would flip the whole verdict to red.

2 / 5 · a11y — can everyone actually use it
[ a11y · must-fix ]
Keyboard trap in the cookie banner
What it means: someone navigating by keyboard (no mouse) presses Tab and gets stuck — focus cycles inside the banner and never escapes. They can't reach the rest of the page at all. Caught by the keyboard probe; axe-core (an automated checker) backs the rest.
2Evidence · assets/axe.json→Fix · trap focus, release on Escape

Any keyboard trap is an automatic must-fix — it locks keyboard-only users out entirely, so it blocks the release.

3 / 5 · journey — does the real flow work on a phone
[ journey · worth-fixing ]
Mobile overflow on signup at 360 px
What it means: on a small phone (360 px wide) the signup page scrolls sideways — content is wider than the screen. To a first-time user that reads as "broken", and it dents their confidence right at the sign-up step. Caught by replaying your written journey at 360 / 768 / 1280.
3Evidence · assets/screenshots/signup-360.png→Fix · constrain the over-wide element to the viewport

The screenshot at that exact width is saved as proof — you can see the overflow, not just read about it.

4 / 5 · vision — is the page overwhelming at a glance
[ vision · worth-fixing · illustrative ]
Cognitive Load Score — busy above the fold
What it means: the Cognitive Load Score (illustrative — the repo ships no sample value; 1–10, where 10 is effortless) is how hard the page is to grasp in a glance. A high score says the top of the page is crowded: too many competing clickable things, dense text, before the user has decided where to look.
4Evidence · agents/vision.md (four-part breakdown)→Fix · thin the above-fold options, lift one clear primary action

A thermometer, not a gate: a high score surfaces and trends in the diff, but never blocks a release on its own.

5 / 5 · copy — can a newcomer actually read it
[ copy · worth-fixing ]
Jargon density 6.2 per 100 words (threshold 5.0)
What it means: roughly one word in sixteen is insider language a first-time customer won't know. The copy agent reads the page like a newcomer — also scoring reading grade (Flesch-Kincaid) and checking trust signals like a regulator line.
5Evidence · assets/copy.json→Fix · swap the flagged terms for plain words

That's what "five specialists" concretely buys you: speed, access, the real flow, the glance, and the words — each with a fix attached.
