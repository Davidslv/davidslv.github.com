SEC A The Story Page 1
The briefing

Operation Nightglass

You were never assigned. There’s no record of you in the building — and there’ll be none when you leave.

Twenty floors stand between the street and the thing they keep at the top. Each one is a grid of guards, cameras and cones of light, and command will swear you were never here.

You carry nothing that can fight. The only tool you’re issued is the dark: read where the light falls, learn the rhythm of a patrol, and move through the gaps it leaves behind.

Get caught, and you were never assigned. Reach the exit, and neither was the asset.

SEC B The Field Manual Page 2
How to play

The field manual

1

Controls

Everything you can do — and the one thing you can’t.
WASD Move — or the Arrow keys
HOLD Wait — let a patrol pass
R Restart the floor
PAD Full gamepad support
And one rule that never changes: there is no combat — you can’t fight a guard, only avoid one.
2

The one rule — light & cones

Light is the enemy. Learn it and the floor is yours.

Guards see in straight cones of light. Stand in a cone and you’re seen; stay in shadow and you’re not.

A guard’s attention climbs a ladder — calm, suspicious, hunting. Read the key below, then read the floor.

CALMGREEN
Hasn’t seen you. A predictable route — read it.
SUSPICIOUSAMBER
A cone clipped you. The guard turns to look.
HUNTINGRED
Seen in full light. The floor goes loud — reset or run.
3

Field tips

Doctrine, pencilled in the margins.
01Never assume a patrol is asleep.
02A closed door is cover; an open one is a sightline.
03The shortest path is almost never the dark one.
04When in doubt, wait — light moves, so can you.
